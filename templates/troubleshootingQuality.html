Troubleshooting Guide: Low Quality Prints

If you are using Ultimaker Cura, before following the steps below go to:

Preferences > Configure Cura > Settings > Check All

To turn on visibility for all settings.

  • Solutions for Under-extrusion:
    • Check filament diameters: materials properties.
      • In Ultimaker Cura: preferences > configure Cura > materials > diameter
    • Increase extrusion multiplier.
      • In Ultimaker Cura: settings > flow rate compensation factor
  • Solutions for Over-extrusion:
    • Check filament diameters: materials properties.
      • In Ultimaker Cura: preferences > configure Cura > materials > diameter
    • Decrease extrusion multiplier.
      • In Ultimaker Cura: settings > flow rate compensation factor
  • Solutions to Stringing/Oozing
    • Retraction Distance
      • Increase retraction distance by 1 mm at a time
      • In Ultimaker Cura: settings > travel > retraction distance
    • Retraction Speed
      • Increase retraction speed
      • Ideal range is 1200-6000 mm/min or 20-100 mm/s
      • Settings > travel > retraction speed
    • Temperature
      • Decrease temperature by 5-10 degrees
      • Settings > materials > printing temperature
    • Long movements over open spaces can cause stringing/oozing
      • Best way to fix this is to change your design: your software should optimize when slicing for the shortest path possible.
    • Movement speed
      • If your printer can handle it, try increasing the speed
      • Tune while print is running and adjust speed
      • Settings > Speed > Print Speed
  • Solutions to Overheating
    • Insufficient Cooling
      • Increase fan power
      • Settings > Cooling >Fan Speed
    • Temperature
      • Decrease temperature by 5-10 degrees
      • Settings > materials > printing temperature
    • Movement Speed
      • Decrease the speed
      • Tune while print is running and adjust speed
      • Settings > Speed > Print Speed
    • Printing multiple parts at once allows for more time to cool
  • Solutions to Layer Shifting
    • Movement Speed
      • Decrease the speed
      • Tune while print is running and adjust speed
      • Settings > Speed > Print Speed
    • Mechanical or Electrical Issues
      • The belt may have stretched over time and may need to be re-tensioned. This process will be different for different printers.
      • The set screw anchoring the pulley to the motor shaft may have loosened.
      • The motors may either be not getting enough electrical current to run at full speed or they are overheating and temporarily shutting down.
  • Solutions to Layer Separation
    • Layer Height
      • Decrease the layer height: the max you should be printing with is 20% smaller than the nozzle size
      • Change layer height: Settings > Quality > Layer Height
      • Check nozzle size. At top of screen to the right of the printer name.
    • Temperature
      • Increase the temperature by 10 degrees: this will allow the plastic to bond better.
      • Settings > material > printing temperature
  • Solutions to Weak infill
    • Infill Pattern
      • Trying different infill patterns may take care of this problem
      • Settings > Infill > Infill Pattern
    • Movement Speed
      • Decrease the speed
      • Tune while print is running and adjust speed
      • Settings > Speed > Print Speed
    • Infill extrusion width
      • Increase the extrusion width of the infill only
      • Settings > Infill > Infill layer thickness
  • Solutions to Scars on top surface
    • See: over-extrusion
    • Vertical lift
      • Nozzle may need to be lifted slightly before moving to its next location to avoid dragging on the surface.
      • Make sure the following settings are checked: Settings > Travel > Avoid printed parts when travelling, avoid supports when travelling, Z Hop when retracted
      • Increase travel avoid distance: Settings > Travel > Travel Avoid Distance
  • Solutions to Elephant foot
    • Build Plate Temperature
      • Try reducing the build plate temperature by 5-10 degrees
      • Settings > Material > Build plate temperature
    • Adhesion
      • Try adding adhesion if you haven't already done so
      • Settings > Build Plate Adhesion > Build Plate Adhesion Type > Brim
  • Solutions to Lines in side of print
    • Inconsistent Extrusion
      • This is caused by filament with an inconsistent width. The best solution is getting higher quality filament.
    • Inconsistent Extruder Temperature
      • Monitor the extruder temperature over the course of your print: if varying cyclically you may need to recalibrate your PID controller
    • Mechanical Issues
      • The print bed may be vibrating or wobbling while printing
      • Z-axis lead screw may be positioned improperly
  • Solutions to Vibrations and Ringing
    • Movement Speed
      • Decrease the speed
      • Tune while print is running and adjust speed
      • Settings > Speed > Print Speed
    • Firmware acceleration
      • If comfortable with you printer's firmware, decreasing the acceleration may help.
    • Mechanical Issues
      • A loose screw or broken bracket could be the cause of this problem.
  • Solutions to Small features not printed
    • Enable Thin Walls
      • Settings > Shell > Print thin walls
    • Part Design
      • The best option for fixing this problem may be simply be redesigning your part to have bigger features.
    • Nozzle Size
      • Nozzle Size
        • You can also install a smaller nozzle on your printer
  • Solutions to Inconsistent extrusion
    • Filament Stuck or Tangled
      • Check to make sure filament is fed unimpeded into the feeder: if the spool rotates freely, if filament is being easily unwound, if filament can easily move through Bowden tube
    • Clogged Extruder
      • Heat extruder and de-clog with thin wire, usually found in maintenance kit
      • If your printer supports it, try doing a hot pull cold pull to clear the nozzle
    • Layer height
      • Try increasing your layer height: inconsistencies can be caused when its too low
      • Settings > Quality > Layer height
    • Inconsistent Filament Width
      • This is caused by poor quality filament. The only solution is to purchase high quality filament.
      • Check the filament width at various places with calipers to see if this is your problem.
  • Solutions to Warping
    • Heated Bed
      • This can be caused by the lack of a heated bed. If possible, install one
      • If you already have a heated bed, try increasing the temperature
    • Adhesion
      • Add adhesion to your part
      • Settings > Build Plate Adhesion > Build plate adhesion type > Brim
    • Fan Cooling
      • You can try disabling fan cooling
      • Settings > Cooling > Enable Print Cooling (uncheck this box)
